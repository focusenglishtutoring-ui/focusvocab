MODULE 6 

anymore (adv.) – 不再
What does it mean?
 Not now, but it was true in the past.
Examples:
 I don’t play that game anymore.
She doesn’t live here anymore.
Do you watch cartoons anymore?
Check your understanding:
 What does anymore mean?
 A) not now, but in the past
 B) very often
 C) in the future

anyone (pron.) – 任何人
What does it mean?
 Any person at all.
Examples:
 Anyone can answer this question.
Did anyone see my bag?
Is anyone ready to start?
Note:
 Anyone means the same as anybody.
Check your understanding:
 What does anyone mean?
 A) any person
 B) one special person
 C) a group of people

anything (pron.) – 任何事物
What does it mean?
 Any thing at all.
Examples:
 You can ask me anything.
Did you eat anything today?
Is there anything I can help with?
Check your understanding:
 What does anything mean?
 A) one special thing
 B) something expensive
 C) any thing at all

anyway (adv.) – 不管怎樣；反正
What does it mean?
 Used when something happens even though there is a problem,
 or when changing the topic.
Examples:
 It was raining, but we went out anyway.
I didn’t like the movie. Anyway, let’s talk about school.
It’s raining, but are you coming anyway?
Check your understanding:
 What does anyway do here?
 A) talks about the future
 B) shows something happens despite a problem
 C) gives an order

anywhere (adv.) – 任何地方
What does it mean?
 In any place.
Examples:
 You can sit anywhere.
I can’t find my keys anywhere.
Did you go anywhere yesterday?
Check your understanding:
 What does anywhere mean?
 A) in one place only
 B) very far away
 C) in any place

apart (adv.) – 分開地
What does it mean?
 Separated, not together.
Examples:
 The two houses are far apart.
Please keep these papers apart.
They live apart now.
Check your understanding:
 What does apart mean?
 A) together
 B) separated
 C) noisy

apart from (prep. phr.) – 除了
What does it mean?
 Used when one thing is not part of the group,
 or when one more thing is added.
Examples:
 Everyone came apart from Tom.
 → Tom did not come.
Apart from English, she studies math.
 → She studies English and math.
Apart from the rain, the trip was great.
 → The trip was great, but the rain was bad.
Check your understanding:
 In the sentence
 “Everyone came apart from Tom,”
 what does it tell you?
 A) Tom came late
 B) Tom came too
 C) Tom did not come

apartment (n.) – 公寓
What does it mean?
 One home inside a big building where many people live.
Examples:
 She lives in an apartment.
Their apartment is on the third floor.
Do you rent an apartment?
Check your understanding:
 What is an apartment?
 A) a whole building
 B) one home in a big building
 C) a hotel room

apartment building (n.) – 公寓大樓
What does it mean?
 A big building with many apartments inside.
Examples:
 That apartment building is very tall.
Many families live in the apartment building.
Is this an apartment building?
Check your understanding:
 What is an apartment building?
 A) one small home
 B) a building with many apartments
 C) a shopping center

app (n.) – 應用程式
What does it mean?
 A program you use on a phone or computer.
Examples:
 I use this app every day.
She downloaded a new app.
Which app do you like most?
Check your understanding:
 What is an app?
 A) a paper form
 B) a kind of website
 C) a program on a phone or computer
Answers:
anymore → A


anyone → A


anything → C


anyway → B


anywhere → C


apart → B


apart from → C


apartment → B


apartment building → B


app → C
